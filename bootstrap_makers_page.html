From earthquakes and wildfires in the West, to hurricanes and floods in the East, natural disasters are occurring more frequently throughout the United States due to the impact of climate change. According to The Economist, losses totaled to $330 billion in 2017. Many residents of these affected areas could have avoided or minimized their losses if they were better informed about the natural disaster risks in the area they live before they decided to live there. Families are uninformed because traditional housing websites do not provide this crucial information to their viewers.

Sahar Sami, Eesha Sharma, Mrinali Nakhre, and Hannah Daniel are high school seniors at the Middlesex County Academy for SMET in Edison, New Jersey. They know that one way to help prospective homeowners would be to help people become more informed the environment where they are hoping to buy.
